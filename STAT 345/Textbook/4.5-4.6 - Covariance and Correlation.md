# Covariance and Correlation
$\text{Cov}(X,Y)=E((X-\mu_X)(Y-\mu_Y))$
$\text{Cov}(X,Y)=E(XY)-\mu_X\mu_Y$

Correlation $\rho_{XY}=\frac{\text{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}$

$\text{Var}(aX+bY)=a^2\text{Var}X+b^2\text{Var}Y+2ab\text{Cov}(X,Y)$

Where the covariance increase when $X$ and $Y$ are more directly related, and will be negative if inversely related. The covariance is 0 if the variables are independent and 1 or -1 if a direct mapping of e
